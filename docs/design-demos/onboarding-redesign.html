Dialed.  Onboarding, redesigned

Proposal · nothing built yet · 2026-07-22

Research first, then a hard audit of what ships today, then this. The goal is one number: minutes from installing Dialed to the first face the user chose being live on their wrist. Full detail lives in docs/ONBOARDING-REDESIGN.md; this page is the visual tour.

2 · Phone — one adaptive Setup screen (replaces the 3-page tour)

It watches the real state of your watch and changes itself, live. Tap the states to see every version of the screen:

Browse faces first ▸
Dialed.
Real faces for your watch.
Nothing leaves your phone.
Why one screen beats three pages: research on first-run flows is blunt — feature tours are skipped or ignored; flows that do the setup instead of describing it activate far more users. This screen performs the connection while the brand moment plays, auto-advances when the watch responds, and always offers a way into the store (“Browse faces first”) so nobody is ever held hostage by hardware.
The new ability underneath: today the phone literally cannot tell “no watch” apart from “watch there, Dialed watch app missing”. The fix uses two signals the app already has (paired-watch list + Dialed-capability list) — their difference is the missing-app state. Once Dialed is on the Play Store, the “Set up my watch” button opens the Play install page on the watch itself (the same mechanism Google’s Horologist library uses), and this screen watches the install complete and moves on by itself.

4 · Watch — one moment instead of two screens

Today the watch shows a permission screen, then a separate “make Dialed your face” screen, and fires the two system dialogs stacked back-to-back with no context. Proposed: one screen, one tap does everything. Step through it:

Why this ordering matters: the second dialog (“set the watch face without asking”) is special — Android lets us ask it exactly once, ever. If it’s denied, it can never be asked again on that watch. Today it fires as a surprise second dialog with zero explanation — the worst possible framing for a one-shot question. Here it arrives one beat after a tap that literally promised “your first face goes on now”, so saying Allow is the obvious, natural answer.
“Later” is safe: it just goes to the watch Home. The ask then happens the next time a face actually arrives — with that face’s name on screen (“Solstice is waiting — allow Dialed to install it”). That’s the strongest permission moment in the whole design, and it happens automatically.
One honest fix in the plumbing: today, if you push a face before the watch app was ever opened, the phone says “your watch is busy — try again”, which is false and unfixable by retrying. One new response code lets the phone say the truth: “Open Dialed on your watch and tap Set up, then push again.” Everything else in the hardened transfer path stays byte-for-byte identical.

6 · What onboarding deliberately does NOT contain

Missing on purposeWhy
Any paywall, price, or “PRO” badgeEvidence: paywalls after a real value moment convert ~2× better than upfront ones. Dialed’s value moment is a face on the wrist. The free faces are the trial; the paywall appears only when a locked face is tapped — always after value.
Account / sign-inThere is nothing to sign into. Absence is a feature.
Notification permissionDialed sends no notifications. Never ask for what you don’t use.
Battery-optimization educationNo background work on the phone. Nothing to educate about.
Phone permissions of any kindThe phone app needs none. One quiet line — “Nothing leaves your phone.” — says it all.

7 · Every failure has a designed answer

SituationWhat the user sees
Watch on old Wear OS (5 or lower)Found out gently during setup, not after a failed push: “runs an older Wear OS… everything on your watch stays as it is” + browse anyway.
No watch paired at allHonest guidance to the pairing app + browse anyway. The store is never locked behind hardware.
Bluetooth off / watch rebootingThe setup card keeps watching and recovers by itself; no error unless the user asks.
Face pushed before the watch app was set upThe new honest sheet (section 5) + the watch shows “{Face} is waiting” when opened.
User denies the one-shot apply permissionNever re-asked (platform rule). Every path lands on the existing 3-step long-press guide.
Reinstalling the app when a face is already on the watchSetup recognises it within ~2 seconds and gets out of the way. No tour replay.
